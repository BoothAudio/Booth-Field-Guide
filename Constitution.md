# The Booth Constitution

## Purpose

The Booth Constitution defines the principles that are considered fundamental to the company.

These principles are intentionally difficult to change.

They exist to ensure that product, engineering, design, marketing, customer support, partnerships, hiring, and leadership all make decisions from the same foundation.

The Constitution does not describe what Booth builds.

It describes how Booth decides.

---

## What belongs here

Only ideas that have repeatedly proven themselves across many independent decisions.

A principle should not be added because it sounds inspiring.

It should be added because reality has demonstrated that Booth consistently behaves this way.

---

## Current Status

Version: 0.1

This document is intentionally incomplete.

Every principle should be considered provisional until it has survived repeated real-world decisions.

---

## Current Principles

### Trust is earned, never claimed.

Trust is Booth's most valuable asset.

Every decision should increase or preserve trust.

---

### Confidence never exceeds evidence.

Booth never presents assumptions as facts.

When uncertainty exists, it should be communicated honestly.

---

### Remove effort, not understanding.

Automation should eliminate unnecessary work while preserving transparency.

Users should understand why Booth reached a conclusion.

---

### Build for decades.

Booth optimizes for long-term trust over short-term metrics.

We would rather grow slowly than compromise the principles that make the company worth building.
